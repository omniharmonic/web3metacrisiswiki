# Information Theory

## Definition

**Information Theory** refers to the mathematical study of information, communication, and data transmission, providing the theoretical foundation for understanding how information is encoded, transmitted, and decoded in digital systems.

## Core Concepts

- **Information**: Mathematical measure of information content
- **Entropy**: Measure of uncertainty or randomness
- **Communication**: Transmission of information
- **Encoding**: Converting information into signals
- **Decoding**: Recovering information from signals

## Technical Mechanisms

### Mathematical Foundations
- **Shannon Entropy**: Measure of information content
- **Mutual Information**: Shared information between variables
- **Channel Capacity**: Maximum information transmission rate
- **Error Correction**: Detecting and correcting transmission errors
- **Compression**: Reducing information redundancy

### Communication Systems
- **Source Coding**: Efficient information encoding
- **Channel Coding**: Error-resistant transmission
- **Modulation**: Converting digital signals to analog
- **Demodulation**: Converting analog signals to digital
- **Synchronization**: Aligning transmitter and receiver

### Information Processing
- **Data Compression**: Reducing data size
- **Error Detection**: Identifying transmission errors
- **Error Correction**: Fixing transmission errors
- **Cryptography**: Secure information transmission
- **Steganography**: Hiding information in other data
