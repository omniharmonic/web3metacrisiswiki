# Phishing

## Definition

**Phishing** refers to a type of cyber attack where attackers impersonate legitimate entities through fraudulent communications (emails, messages, websites) to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, credit card numbers, or other personal data.

## Core Concepts

- **Impersonation**: Pretending to be legitimate organizations or individuals
- **Social Engineering**: Exploiting human psychology and trust
- **Information Theft**: Stealing sensitive personal and financial information
- **Deception**: Using false pretenses to gain access to information
- **Urgency**: Creating false urgency to pressure victims into quick decisions

## Technical Mechanisms

### Attack Vectors
- **Email Phishing**: Fraudulent emails impersonating legitimate organizations
- **SMS Phishing (Smishing)**: Phishing attacks via text messages
- **Voice Phishing (Vishing)**: Phishing attacks via phone calls
- **Website Phishing**: Fake websites designed to steal information
- **Social Media Phishing**: Phishing attacks through social media platforms

### Information Gathering
- **Targeting**: Identifying potential victims through various means
- **Reconnaissance**: Gathering information about targets
- **Spoofing**: Creating fake but convincing communications
- **Malware**: Distributing malicious software through phishing
- **Data Harvesting**: Collecting stolen information for further attacks
