# Social Credit Systems

## Definition

**Social Credit Systems** refer to comprehensive digital surveillance and scoring systems that monitor, evaluate, and rate individuals' behavior, social interactions, and compliance with societal norms, enabling social control, discrimination, and manipulation of access to services and opportunities.

## Core Concepts

- **Social Scoring**: Numerical rating of individuals based on behavior and compliance
- **Behavioral Monitoring**: Continuous monitoring of individual behavior and activities
- **Social Control**: Using scores to control access to services and opportunities
- **Discrimination**: Discriminatory treatment based on social credit scores
- **Privacy Violation**: Massive invasion of personal privacy
- **Autonomy Erosion**: Undermining individual autonomy and free will

## Technical Mechanisms

### Scoring Systems
- **Algorithmic Scoring**: AI-powered scoring of individual behavior
- **Real-Time Monitoring**: Continuous monitoring and score updates
- **Cross-Platform Integration**: Integration across multiple systems and platforms
- **Predictive Analytics**: Using data to predict future behavior and compliance
- **Automated Decision Making**: AI-driven automated decisions based on scores
- **Social Network Analysis**: Analyzing social connections and interactions

### Data Collection and Processing
- **Behavioral Data**: Collection of behavioral and social interaction data

- **Financial Data**: Monitoring financial transactions and behavior
- **Location Tracking**: GPS and location-based monitoring
- **Biometric Data**: Biometric identification and monitoring
- **Cross-Platform Tracking**: Tracking across multiple platforms and systems
