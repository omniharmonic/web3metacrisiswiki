# Interoperability

## Definition

**Interoperability** is the capacity of blockchain systems to work together seamlessly, enabling data, assets, and services to move across different blockchain networks and platforms without friction, creating a unified ecosystem of interconnected systems.

## Core Concepts

- **Cross-Chain Operations**: Operations that work across different blockchains
- **Data Portability**: Moving data between different systems
- **Asset Transfer**: Transferring assets between different blockchains
- **Service Integration**: Integrating services across different platforms
- **Unified Standards**: Common standards for interoperability

### Cross-Chain Infrastructure
- **Bridges**: Connecting different blockchain networks
- **Relays**: Relaying information between blockchains
- **Sidechains**: Secondary blockchains connected to main chains
- **Layer 2 Solutions**: Scaling solutions that work across chains
- **Cross-Chain Protocols**: Protocols for cross-chain operations

### Data Standards
- **Common Formats**: Standardized data formats
